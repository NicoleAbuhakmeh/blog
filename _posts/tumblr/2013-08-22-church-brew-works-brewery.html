Church Brew Works sits in a renovated church just outside of the city in Lawrenceville. I wasn’t exactly sure what to expect when I opened the door, but was pleasantly surprised when I did.  They didn’t renovate the structure of the church it’s self, just re-purposed the old building to fit their needs. The pews were shortened to become seating for diners. Leftover pews were fashioned into a bar to serve Church Brew Works crafts.  By far the best part of the renovating was making the Alter where most of the brewing of the beer is done.  

We went to the church on Sunday (something you won’t hear me say often) and got to experience another unique part of their dining menu.  They have a chef’s creative day menu on most Sundays. You can pick a protein, (I went with salmon) pick a style, (I went with asian) and tell them anything you don’t like. The chef then creates you something unique from that structure for your meal. I got a Honey-Soy Grilled Salmon with Sliced Red Beets over Pilaf.  It was a risk for me as I usually play it safe with something I know i’ll like.  Overall it was a truly a unique experience.  

The beer I sampled on this day included: 

  • Millrnnium Trippel
  • Cherry Quadzilla
  • Celestial Gold
  • Pipe Organ Pale Ale
  • Pious Monk Dunkel
  • Thunderhop IPA
  • Heavenly Hefeweizen
  • Coconut Stout
