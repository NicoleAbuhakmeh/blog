Back in August I took a road trip down to check out a few of the brewery offerings in Northern Virginia or NoVa for the locals.   Our first stop of the day was in Leesburg.

Vintage 50.

It was neatly tucked away behind a strip mall off the main road. I wasn’t sure what to expect walking in through the lobby doors of a business center, but then as we turned the corner into the brewery the hops hit me nose and I knew we were in the right place.  The restaurant has an outdoor seating area that seems like it would be the perfect place for an blowing-off-steam-after-work Happy Hour or inside the grand bar area for a more upscale event. 

I decided to kick off my tasting with a sampler of: 

  • Old Carolina Roggen
  • Duex Mois Biere De Garde (Top Notch!)
  • Anonymous Dampf
  • 50 CAT IPA

After we put in our order- I moseyed on over to take a peek inside the brewhouse, (it’s slightly quartered off from the restaurant) and I met Vintage 50's Brewster Bret Kimbrough.  

One of the best parts of writing about craft beer is meeting the people behind it. I love when it works out that I can visit a brewery on a weekday. Watching them do what they do and seeing the pride they take into the beer they craft makes me even prouder to buy it. 

Over the smell the chocolate and coffee brewing in the background for their Percheron Porter, Bret welcomed us to the brewery and told us a little about what they had going in fermentation next.  I was sad to have missed any of their darker beers being available on tap, but then again it was the middle of summer. Then again, it can’t be just me that likes a good Stout on a hot summer day, can it?

Take in a visit to Vintage 50 the next time you’re around the area.
