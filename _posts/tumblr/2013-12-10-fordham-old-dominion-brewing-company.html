“They can only say No.” That’s the lesson i’ve learned in 2013. Well, that and to stop being afraid of rejection. Maybe that’s a little deep for a brewery post, but it fits for this occasion.

I tend to pack my roadtrips with all the breweries in the area. I want to try them all.  Recently on a trip down to Punkin-Chuckin’ weekend in Delaware, I put Fordham - Old Dominion Brewing Company on my must visit list.  Unfortunately, I found out they would be closed during the time I was passing through. This is where the awesome staff of their brewery stepped in and told me just to say the time I would like to come in and they would be there to greet us. (Thanks Twitter, owe you one!)

Not only did they have their tasting room open, but Eric the tasting room manager gave us a private tour.  I was really blown away by their hospitality.  We got the back story of the 2 breweries becoming 1 in 2007, How they have a huge following in England- thanks to the exporting of their year round staples, and sampled some tasty brews including:  

  • Copperhead Ale
  • Helles Lager
  • Wisteria Wheat
  • Spiced Harvest Ale (previously enjoyed)
  • Hop Mountain
  • Gigi’s Farmhouse Ale
  • Double D (previously enjoyed)
  • Oak Barrel Stout (previously enjoyed)
  • Candi

Candi, the last one on the list is a sassy little Belgian-style Tripel Brewed with Belgian Candi Sugar. Yum. (Try not to reach through the screen.)

They also make craft sodas and have festivals at their location in Dover. Next year - Cheesetoberfest - i’m totally there! Be sure to look for that upcoming event on their website:
